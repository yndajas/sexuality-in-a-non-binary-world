--- title: "Closing thoughts" layout: default previous: "relanguaging-sexuality" next: "references" ---
  • 'Queer' is a great catch-all, recognising that sexuality is complicated and messy, but it has limitations:
    • Obfuscation of what's going on underneath
    • Lack of visibility for less well known or understood sexualities
  • Specifity and more open discussion about what lies beneath sexuality can counter that
  • However, care must be taken over the dangers of specificity. Preference can open up potential for prejudice:
    • Morphology: ableism, fatphobia and racism
    • Aesthetics: femmephobia
    • Character: ableism (e.g. sapiosexual)
  • Intersectional perspectives are vital when considering linguistic innovation
