Assumptions

  • "Sexual people can experience sexual attraction to people based purely on looking at them" (Jas 2020: 78)
  • Trans women, trans men and non-binary people exist
  • Medical ‘transition’ is optional
  • "[I]dentity, aesthetics and morphology need not align with the norms of gender-conforming cis people. Rather, they have infinite potential combinations" (Jas 2020: 78):
    • "[F]emme-presenting men who have protruded chests, sometimes hidden by a binder or tape"
    • "[W]omen who present neither femme nor masc - or perhaps both - and have a flat chest"
    • "[M]asc-presenting non-binary people with large hips who have had a breast reduction"

Problems

  • "[W]ho or what are (sexual) lesbian, gay and straight people attracted to if women and men can look like anything both clothed and unclothed?" (Jas 2020: 78)
  • "Do these categories - under their traditional definitions - retain meaning (or semantic coherence) when we include trans people as potential subjects of sexual attraction?" (Jas 2020: 78)
