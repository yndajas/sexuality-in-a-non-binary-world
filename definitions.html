Gender

  • Bornstein (2016: 28):
    • Being: "[g]ender identity answers the question: 'Who am I?' Am I a man or a woman or a what?"
    • Belonging: "feeling like you belong with people of a particular gender or genders (more than other genders)" (Jas 2020: 73)
  • "[F]elt sense of gender" (Vincent 2018: 18)
  • Self-evaluation against own sense of what different gender categories mean (to you)

Gender system

The article Gender beyond the binary: visualisation, language and conceptual frameworks outlines my attempt to develop a more comprehensive 'system' of gender and associated categories, including its motivations and a method of mapping gender. Below is a 'system' of gender discussed in that article.

Sexuality

  • "[S]exual orientation concerns who one wants to go to bed with, whilst gender identity is who one goes to bed as" (Vincent 2018: 19; emphasis original)
  • Sexuality is more than sexual orientation. Van Anders (2015: 1178), in proposing 'Sexual Configurations Theory', wrote:
"Sexual orientation as defined by gender (or is it sex?) is largely positioned as the singular defining feature of people's sexual selves, but should it be? There are a number of other axes along which sexuality could revolve, including age, partner number, type of sexual activity, consent, solitary sexuality, and intensity among others."
  • However, focus here is on orientation and what lies beneath

Arousal, attraction and desire

  • Arousal: sexual excitement
  • Attraction: appreciation of specific qualities
  • Desire: want for sex

Queer

My position, somewhat in contrast to orthodox queer theory:

  • Queer can be many things, including an identity category (not anti-identity)
  • Support for subjective, non-prescriptive definition
  • However, identity categories are not everything: individuals are more than the groups to which they belong
