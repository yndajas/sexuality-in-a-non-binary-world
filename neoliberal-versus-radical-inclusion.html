Tinder and OkCupid: ownership, approaches and press

  • Tinder: same owner as OkCupid, almost identical approach. Tinder however uses the language of "show" (on profile) versus "include" (in searches) - genders beyond the binary can be shown but not included
  • Showing is enough to get good press. One of many example articles reads (Smith 2014):
    • "OKCupid [sic] Just Exploded The Gender Binary With a Huge New Range of Orientation Options"
    • "OKCupid [...] has just laid down the gauntlet when it comes to inclusivity"
    • "[T]he changes that OKCupid has just started rolling out to users are revolutionary"

Barriers

  • Types of inclusion:
    • Liberal: making small changes within the existing framework
    • Radical: changing the system
  • Liberal approach of OkCupid, Tinder and many organisations beyond the dating app domain highlights three major barriers:
    • Willingness to accept/tolerate, but not challenge underlying assumptions about gender and the world (Nicholas 2019)
    • "The ubiquity of binary gender in how we conceptualise sexuality" (Jas 2020: 76). Updating search algorithms may create work for users in (re)evaluating who they see as potential matches, leading to ontological instability
    • "[T]he neoliberal agenda, which puts profit over people (Chomsky 1999)" (Jas 2020: 76). It's profitable to look progressive, less so to work out the details, and potentially confuse the core userbase of cisgender straight and LGB people by calling into question those very categories
  • Companies that do this kind of virtue signalling often receive significant praise, which makes it harder for community members to be heard when calling for more meaningful changes
