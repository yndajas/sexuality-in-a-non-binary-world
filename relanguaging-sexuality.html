  • Gender is more complicated than traditional sexuality categories can account for
  • "[W]e cannot consistently accurately identify the gender of individuals" (Jas 2020: 85)
  • So "how can we describe what we are attracted to and aroused by without relying on essentialist understandings of identity?" (Jas 2020: 86)

Identity

  • If we include fetishes, identity could be a form of idea-based arousal or attraction to:
    • a specific gender (androsexual, gynosexual and skoliosexual)
    • sameness (gay)
    • difference (straight)
  • If fetishes are out, "such labels fall down at the 'Who are guys?' question" (Jas 2020: 86)

Morphology

  • Again rests on sexuality-fetish distinction
  • Sex, like gender, is complicated and not truly binary in nature (Blackless et al. 2000; Vincent 2018) - AFAB and AMAB are insufficient as a proxy for morphology)
  • Categorisation is difficult - describe specific aspects of morphology?

Aesthetics and character

  • 'Femme' and 'masc' are popular, but there are issues:
    • Still a binary that can't capture the true diversity of expression
    • "[H]ighly culturally-contextualised [and] subject to individual interpretation" (Jas 2020: 87)
  • More specificity; potential for more nuanced neologisms?
